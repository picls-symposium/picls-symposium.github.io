Prof. Scott Waddell
  • Follow:

Department of Physiology, Anatomy and Genetics, University of Oxford

Biography

Scott Waddell studied biochemistry as an undergraduate at the University of Dundee, and researched cancer biology for his Ph.D. at the University of London. After postdoctoral study in the Department of Brain and Cognitive Sciences at Massachusetts Institute of Technology he spent 10 years leading a research group in the Department of Neurobiology at the University of Massachusetts Medical School. He arrived in Oxford as a Professor of Neurobiology, Wellcome Senior Research Fellow in Basic Biomedical Sciences and founding member of the Centre for Neural Circuits & Behaviour in November 2011. He is also co-Director of the Oxford Martin School’s Programme on Mind and Machine.

 

Drosophila neurobiology

The Waddell group studies Drosophila as a model to understand how internal states, memory and sensory stimuli are integrated to produce behaviour. Genetic techniques allow temporal control of neural circuitry in behaving flies. Among other things, they have defined small groups of neurons controlling memory consolidation and shown that dopamine has a role in reward and motivation in flies, as it does in mammals. Recently they have shown that, like in mammals, fly neural genomes are mosaic due to active transposition – potentially contributing to individual differences in brain function and behaviour. Their findings emphasise the cellular and mechanistic resolution understanding of conserved brain properties that can be achieved using Drosophila.

About

The College of Life Sciences’ PhD Association (PiCLS) within the University of Dundee was established in 2008 to allow networking between PhD students in different subject areas. In 2010 the PiCLS symposium was introduced and was such a great success that the event is now an annual symposium run entirely by students.
